Friday — Dedicated Cardio & Conditioning Day
~60 min
Zone 2 base HIIT intervals LT threshold
No resistance training. This session builds aerobic capacity, fat metabolism, and recovery ability for HYROX + strength training.
Full session structure — 60 min
Zone 2
1. Warm-up — treadmill or stationary bike
10 min · Easy pace

Treadmill: 4.5–6 km/h walk (light incline 0–2%)
Bike: light resistance, 60–70 RPM

Goal: Raise heart rate gradually + nasal breathing
HIIT
2. Intervals — treadmill or stationary cycle
Equipment: Stationary Cycle (preferred) or Treadmill

Wk 1–2: 4 × 90 sec hard effort · 2 min easy recovery
Wk 3–4: 5 × 2 min hard effort · 2 min recovery
Wk 5+: 6 × 2 min hard effort · 2 min recovery

Execution:
Stationary Cycle:
• Hard phase → high resistance, 80–100 RPM (breathing heavy but controlled)
• Recovery → very light resistance, easy spin (60–70 RPM)

Treadmill:
• Hard phase → 10–14 km/h (fast run, NOT sprint)
• Recovery → 4.5–6 km/h (brisk walk)

Goal: Maintain same output across all intervals without drop in pace or form
LT Work
3. Lactate threshold block
10 min continuous steady work

Treadmill: 6.5–9 km/h @ 5–8% incline
• 6.5–7.5 km/h → beginner (power walk)
• 7.5–8.5 km/h → intermediate (fast incline walk / light jog)
• 8.5–9 km/h → advanced steady run

Goal: Sustain discomfort without stopping (“comfortably hard”)
Zone 2
4. Cool-down
8 min easy walk or cycling

Treadmill: 4.5–5.5 km/h flat walk
Bike: very light resistance, 50–60 RPM

Goal: Drop heart rate + restore nasal breathing
Progression law: Never increase interval intensity before improving recovery. You progress only when HR consistently returns below baseline within recovery window.
