Friday — Structured Cardio & Conditioning
~60 min
Zone 2 HIIT intervals
Dedicated conditioning day — no resistance training. Zone 2 base first to prime aerobic pathways, then HIIT intervals for VO2max stimulus. Lactate threshold block between them forces adaptation at the aerobic-anaerobic boundary.
Full session structure
Zone 2
Warm-up — Row erg or treadmill
10 min · 60–65% HRmax · Comfortable conversational pace
HIIT
Intervals — Ski erg or assault bike
Wk 1–2: 4×90s @ 85–90% HRmax · 2 min Z2 recovery
Wk 3–4: 5×2 min @ 85–92% HRmax · 2 min Z2 recovery
Wk 5: 6×2 min @ 88–92% HRmax · 2 min Z2 recovery
LT Work
Lactate threshold block — Treadmill
10 min continuous · 78–83% HRmax · Incline 5–8% · Challenging but sustainable
Zone 2
Cool-down — Row or walk
8 min · 55–60% HRmax · Parasympathetic recovery
Progression logic: Start with 4 intervals — do not begin at 6. The aerobic base is currently untrained. Adding intensity before base capacity develops creates fatigue accumulation, not adaptation. Let HRmax recovery between sets get faster over weeks — that IS the adaptation signal.
